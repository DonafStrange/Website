About Me

I am a researcher in computational neuroscience with a focus on understanding how brain dynamics relate to behavior, cognition, and neurotechnology applications. My work spans brain modeling, EEG analysis, and brain-computer interfaces designing, combining theoretical frameworks with real-world neural data.

Currently, I am a Junior Research Fellow at IIT Jodhpur, where I contribute to modeling brain activity using large-scale oscillator networks and connectome-based simulations. I have also worked on decoding attention and emotional states from EEG during naturalistic smartphone use, using both classical machine learning and deep learning methods.

With a strong interdisciplinary background, I aim to bridge the gap between brain modeling and applied neurotechnology, ultimately contributing to systems for diagnosis, intervention, and brain-computer communication.
